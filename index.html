Box Office Economics: Budget Versus Gross Earnings in Cinema

Budget and Gross Earning in the context of the movie industry are related. The budget of a movie represents the amount of money invested in producing it, while Gross Earning represents how much revenue the movie generated at the box office. These two metrics are interconnected because the financial success of a movie is often measured by comparing its Gross Earnings to its Budget.
